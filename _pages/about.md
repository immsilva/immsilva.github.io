<!-- # About -->

I am a Professor (Professor Auxiliar) at the [Department of Informatics](https://www.di.uminho.pt) of the [University of Minho](https://www.uminho.pt/) and a Senior [Researcher](https://algoritmi.uminho.pt/user/ivo-miguel-menezes-silva/) at the [ALGORITMI Research Center](https://algoritmi.uminho.pt). My academic work combines teaching, research, and the supervision of students in areas related to positioning, informatics, and intelligent systems.

I completed my PhD in Telecommunications in 2022 through the MAP-tele Doctoral Programme, a joint doctoral programme of the Universities of Minho, Aveiro, and Porto, following an MSc in Telecommunications and Informatics Engineering from the University of Minho in 2016. My academic work sits at the intersection of telecommunications, computer science, and applied digital technologies, with an emphasis on developing and evaluating practical solutions for positioning, navigation, and sensing problems.

My main activities include teaching in higher education, conducting research, collaborating in academic and applied R&D projects, and supervising students. As a professor, I am interested in helping students build a strong technical foundation while connecting theory with practical problem solving. As a researcher, I focus on application-oriented methods and systems, prioritizing experimental validation, realistic evaluation settings, and the study of real-world applicability in operational environments.

## Research interests

My research interests include topics such as:

- Indoor positioning and indoor navigation
- Positioning and localization for autonomous and cooperative systems
- GNSS and vehicle positioning in challenging environments
- Sensor fusion for navigation and localization
- Wi-Fi fingerprinting and radio-based positioning
- Mobile computing, urban computing, and smart environments
- Internet of Things and sensing systems
- Simulation tools and synthetic data generation for indoor and urban scenarios
- Experimental evaluation of positioning systems in real-world and application-driven contexts

More broadly, I am interested in research that combines technical rigor with practical relevance, particularly in the design, implementation, and evaluation of systems that can be deployed and assessed under realistic operating conditions, including collaborations involving academia, industry, and public-sector applications.

I also have a strong interest in promoting open science practices, particularly through the sharing of research data, code, materials, and evaluation resources that support transparency, reproducibility, and the broader reuse of scientific results.
